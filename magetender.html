About

MageTender is a 2D pixel art fantasy bartending game where players craft drinks to guide a customer’s mood toward a target outcome. Each ingredient shifts emotional values, turning the drink-making process into a small strategy puzzle.

Trailer

Each customer enters with a current mood and a desired state. After a short interaction, the player mixes ingredients that push those values in different directions, aiming to get as close as possible to the target with a single drink.

During development, I focused on UI and overall clarity. Through playtesting, we found that players understood the goal but struggled to interpret how their choices affected the result. In response, I designed and iterated on a mood graph UI that clearly displays both current and target states, making the system more readable, especially for new players.

As the project has progressed, I've taken on a broader role across design, marketing, and organization. I help guide the game’s direction, coordinate team meetings, and shape how the project is presented externally, with trailers and short videos.

Takeaways

Through this project, I’ve learned the importance of consistent playtesting and iteration, and using player feedback to guide design decisions early and often. Working within a larger team has had its own learning curve as well, but through weekly standups and well-structured meetings, we have been making good progress on a really cool project. As the game evolved, I expanded from the design lead role into marketing and presentation as well, taking additional online courses in digital marketing to better support how the game is shared online. I've learned a great amount about the different departments that work together to bring a game to life.

I am excited to announce that MageTender will be on Steam this summer!
