About This Project

“Infinite Camping World” is an interactive procedural map built with p5.js that generates an endlessly scrolling isometric landscape. The terrain is created using seeded noise (p5.js Perlin noise), so each world seed produces a consistent arrangement of water, grass, and dirt while still feeling organic.

As you explore, the world populates with natural details like trees, flowers, and grass, and you can click to place tents on valid land tiles. Arrow keys or WASD move the camera, letting you navigate the environment and experiment with different seeds and layouts.

This piece is part of a three-part interactive series focused on sustainability: responsible camping practices, the impact of deforestation, and the long-term process of reforestation. Together, the projects explore how procedural systems can communicate environmental change through playful interaction.
