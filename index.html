About Me

I’m a Computer Science: Game Design student at UC Santa Cruz with a strong interest in both game design and the business side of games, especially marketing, player research, and how games connect with their communities.

Most of my experience comes from team-based game projects, where I’ve taken on a mix of roles across design, UI, implementation, organization, and presentation. I really enjoy figuring out how all of the moving parts of a project fit together, and I’ve learned a lot through collaboration, playtesting, and iteration.

On my current project, MageTender, I originally pitched the idea and now help out across design, marketing, and team organization as we work toward releasing the game on Steam. That process has made me even more interested in how games are not only made, but also how they are presented, shared, and understood by players.

I’m especially interested in opportunities where I can work closely with teams, learn more about player behavior, and contribute to both the experience of a game and how it reaches its audience.
