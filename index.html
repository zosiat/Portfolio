About

I am a Computer Science: Game Design student interested in building interactive systems and procedural tools for games and creative media. My background combines programming with game design, which shapes how I approach both technical and visual work.

Much of my recent work focuses on procedural generation. I am interested in how generative systems can create variation, support exploration, and help communicate ideas that are difficult to express through static content alone.

I am particularly drawn to projects that use games and interactive media for education and for raising awareness around environmental issues. In these contexts, systems-based design can help players understand scale, constraints, and long-term effects through interaction rather than explanation.

This portfolio includes research on procedural content generation with large language models, as well as creative coding projects that explore atmosphere, motion, and environmental variation.
